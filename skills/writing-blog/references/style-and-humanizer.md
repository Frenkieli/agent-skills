# Style And Humanizer Pass

Use this reference when rewriting, polishing, or doing the final prose pass on a
blog/article.

## Style Target

The target style is rigorous narrative: the structure and logic are rigorous,
while the prose can stay relaxed and personal.

The article should not read like a technical report generated by AI. Preserve
the author's personal judgment, concrete experience, emotional stance, and
natural phrasing when they are part of the source.

## Prose Rules

- Prefer natural direct wording over academic register.
- Use shorter sentences when a sentence forces the reader to hold several
  clauses, conditions, or actors at once.
- Make subjects explicit, especially in multi-step processes.
- Avoid passive voice when it hides the actor.
- Do not overuse bold. Use bold for core conclusions, not every keyword.
- Avoid inflated symbolic language, promotional wording, vague attribution,
  symmetrical three-part routines, and generic high-polish AI phrasing.

## Expectation Management

- Headings must preview the actual content at the right abstraction level.
- The first sentence of a section should carry the reader from the previous
  section into the new one.
- Paragraph endings should set up the next paragraph.
- Technical articles usually do not need suspense. State the conclusion first,
  then support it.
- Turns and conclusions need setup. If the text says "now I realize X," the
  reader needs to know what changed from the earlier view.

## Final Pass

Before finalizing a rewrite:

1. Check whether the output added unsupported claims.
2. Check whether the author's original judgment is still traceable.
3. Remove mechanical AI phrasing without making the article vague or overly
   casual.
4. Keep the requested structure and scope. Do not add extra sections for polish.
